In Spring 2018, Peter Hall, a senior lecturer and course leader at Central Saint Martins University of the Arts in London, came to UConn to hold a workshop based around his in progress essay, "Rewrite". Our class was to read the book and pick a phrase or sentence to design.
I chose the sentence "When words are written over, crossed out and marked for deletion, their traces remain present; historically when substrates used for writing were rare and expensive like vellum, papyrus and parchment, text considered obsolete or heretical were erased and written over". I focused on the idea of crossing things out and having things hidden from view. I used watercolor to represent the idea of rewriting over by having colors mix and used tape as a way to cross out and overlap words.
